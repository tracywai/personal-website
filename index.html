About Me

I’m a student, early-career product manager, and possibly one of the biggest extroverts you'll meet.

Currently studying Systems Design Engineering @ UWaterloo. When I’m not knee deep in non-fiction, catch me going on long walks or playing word games! I can also be found watching NBA and NCAA Women's basketball, learning a new viola concerto, or exploring new cities with my friends.

I always feel at home meeting people who are curious, candid, and infectiously driven. Whether this sounds like you or who you're striving to be, reach out and let’s chat!

Some of my favourite reads are...

When Breath Becomes Air by Paul Kalanithi

  • Memoir

One of the first memoirs I've read. Paul eloquently narrates his battle with cancer, which had been concurrent with his early career in neurosurgery. An incredibly moving memoir and my go-to non-fiction recommendation.

Inspired by Marty Cagan

  • Product Management

The book that grew my love for product management. I especially liked the product manager profiles from a wide range of companies that details how the decisions were made and its lasting impact.

They Said This Would Be Fun by Eternity Martis

  • Memoir

At the height of BLM, I read this memoir and referenced it as the basis for my (very lengthy) essay spotlighting social justice issues at Canadian institutions. You can read it here.

I'm Glad My Mom Died by Jennette McCurdy

  • Memoir

I grew up watching both iCarly and Sam and Cat, and to learn what Jennette was going through was quite an emotional rollercoaster. She articulates her relationship with acting really well and I appreciated how she integrated her sense of humour!

Educated by Tara Westover

  • Memoir

I really admired Tara's drive to pursue her own path with education and her bravery to tell her story. She's a talented writer and conveys the complexity in her familial relationships quite well and I was thoroughly captivated thorughout the entire book.
